AABB

Axis Aligned Bounding Box | bounding parallelepiped, justified on parallel axes
The area in the space surrounding a certain object, in the form of a rectangular parallelepiped with the parties, parallel to the coordinate axes in the world system. When turning the object of AABB modifies its dimensions, but always remains focused on the axes. The second version of The AABB - the cube with the parties, equal to the maximum size of the object: thus, the AABB does not need to be converted to the transformation of the object, and in any position of the object does not go beyond the AABB, although, of course, with the loss of accuracy.
AABB is used in areas such as the partitioning of the space, collision detection and tracing.

See also the OBB, Collision Detection.
